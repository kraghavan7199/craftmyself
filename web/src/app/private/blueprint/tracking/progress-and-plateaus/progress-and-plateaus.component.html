Track Everything: The Key to Progress

A Reality Check on Body Fat Testing

Be wary of relying on methods like DXA scans or skinfold calipers to track short-term progress. Even lab-grade methods have significant error rates (Â±2-10%) that can be larger than the real changes you're making week-to-week. An inaccurate test can mislead you into making poor adjustments. Use the practical tools below instead.

Daily Weigh-Ins

Weigh yourself daily and take a weekly average to track the true trend, ignoring daily fluctuations.

Weekly Measurements

Measure waist, hips, chest, and limbs weekly. A shrinking waist with stable/growing limbs is a reliable sign of good recomposition.

Weekly Photos

Take front, side, and back pictures weekly in consistent lighting. Photos reveal visual changes that numbers can miss.

Gym Performance

Are you getting stronger in your target rep ranges? This is a key indicator of lean mass gain or retention.

Breaking Through Plateaus

Stalled Fat Loss?

If weekly average weight and waist haven't dropped for 2-3 weeks:

  • 1.Ensure your activity and tracking are consistent.
  • 2.Reduce daily intake by 100-200 calories (from carbs/fats) OR add 1-2 moderate cardio sessions.
  • 3.Hold for 2-3 weeks before adjusting again. Consider a diet break if you've been dieting for a long time.

Stalled Muscle Gain?

If weekly average weight and strength haven't increased for 2-3 weeks:

  • 1.Increase daily intake by 100-200 calories (from carbs/fats).
  • 2.Monitor for 2-3 weeks before adding more calories.
  • 3.Ensure your training intensity and progression are on point.
