Performance Metrics: Quantifying Your Progress

To make intelligent adjustments, you need objective data. These tools go beyond the mirror to provide a clearer picture of your performance and guide your training.

The RPE & RIR Scale: Mastering Autoregulation

Rate of Perceived Exertion (RPE) and Reps in Reserve (RIR) are tools to regulate your training intensity based on how you feel on a given day, accounting for stress, sleep, and recovery.

Establishing Baselines & Testing Progress

Strength Testing

Use an "Estimated 1 Rep Max" (e1RM) from a set of 3-5 reps to safely gauge your strength. Re-test every 8-12 weeks to ensure your program is working. Sites like Symmetric Strength provide objective benchmarks based on your sex and weight to identify weaknesses.

Mobility & Flexibility Testing

Periodically film yourself doing simple tests to track progress. Examples: A bodyweight overhead squat (assesses ankle, hip, and thoracic mobility) or a simple toe-touch (assesses hamstring and lower back flexibility).

Velocity-Based Training (VBT)

A highly advanced method using devices to measure bar speed. It provides the most objective measure of intensity and fatigue, allowing you to autoregulate with extreme precision (e.g., terminating a set when bar speed drops below a certain threshold).
