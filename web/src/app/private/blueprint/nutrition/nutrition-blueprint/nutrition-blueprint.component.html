The Nutrition Blueprint

Energy Balance is King, Macros are Queen

For changing weight, how much you eat is key. For changing body composition, what you eat is crucial.

Step 1: Set Your Calories (TDEE)

Your Total Daily Energy Expenditure is your calorie budget. Use an online calculator or the example below to find your starting point.

  • To Cut (Fat Loss): Eat at a 20-25% calorie deficit (for a 0.5-1.0% weekly BW loss).
  • To Lean Bulk: Eat at a 5-15% calorie surplus (for a 0.25-1.5% monthly BW gain, based on experience).

Step 2: Set Your Macros

Distribute your calories to optimize body composition.

  • Protein (Cut): 1.0-1.2g per lb of BW.
  • Protein (Bulk): 0.7-1.0g per lb of BW.
  • Fat: 20-30% of total calories.
  • Carbs: Fill the remaining calories.

Practical Example: Calorie & Macro Calculation

Let's calculate for a hypothetical 180 lb male who exercises 4x per week and wants to cut fat.

  1. 1
    Estimate TDEE: A quick rule of thumb for an active person is bodyweight (lbs) x 15.
    180 lbs × 15 = 2700 calories/day (maintenance).
  2. 2
    Set Calorie Goal (Deficit): For a 20% deficit, we multiply the TDEE by 0.8.
    2700 × 0.8 = 2160 calories/day. Let's round to 2200 calories.
  3. 3
    Set Protein (Cutting): Aim for 1g per lb of bodyweight.
    180g of Protein. Since protein has 4 calories per gram: 180 × 4 = 720 calories.
  4. 4
    Set Fat: Aim for 25% of total calories. Fat has 9 calories per gram.
    2200 × 0.25 = 550 calories. 550 ÷ 9 ≈ 60g of Fat.
  5. 5
    Set Carbs: Fill the rest of the calories. Carbs have 4 calories per gram.
    2200 - 720 (protein) - 550 (fat) = 930 calories. 930 ÷ 4 ≈ 230g of Carbs.
Cutting Macros: ~2200 Calories | 180g Protein | 60g Fat | 230g Carbs

Practical Fueling & Health

Sample Food Lists

  • Lean Proteins: Chicken Breast, Turkey, Fish, Lean Beef, Eggs, Greek Yogurt
  • Complex Carbs: Oats, Brown Rice, Quinoa, Potatoes, Whole-Wheat Bread
  • Healthy Fats: Avocado, Nuts, Seeds, Olive Oil, Fatty Fish (Salmon)
  • Fruits & Veggies: Berries, Bananas, Spinach, Broccoli, Leafy Greens

Meal Timing & Frequency

  • Meal Frequency: 3-6 meals/day is ideal for most. It helps manage hunger without constant food focus.
  • Peri-Workout: The "anabolic window" is overhyped. Simply have a protein-rich meal 1-3 hours before and after training. Pre-workout carbs can help performance.
  • Alcohol's Impact: Provides empty calories, hinders protein synthesis, and can disrupt sleep. Minimize intake for best results.

The Non-Negotiables

  • Hydration: Aim for ~1L per 23kg of bodyweight (or 2/3 of BW in lbs as oz). A simpler guide: drink to thirst and aim for light-colored urine.
  • Fiber: Aim for ~14g per 1000 calories. This supports gut health and satiety. Max out at ~20% of total carbs to avoid GI distress.
  • Micronutrients: Eat a variety of fruits and vegetables. Aim for at least 1 serving of each per 1000kcals when bulking, and per 500kcals when cutting.

Strategic Considerations

Strategic Decision: Gain or Cut?

For non-novices, start a gaining phase when body fat is below ~15% (men) or ~23% (women). This minimizes the length of future cuts. Aim for at least a 4:1 ratio of time spent gaining vs. cutting.

The Role of Cardio

Use cardio as a tool to help create a deficit, but don't overdo it. Excessive cardio can interfere with strength/hypertrophy adaptations. Prioritize low-impact forms and keep total cardio time to less than half your weekly lifting time.

Energy Availability (RED-S)

Prolonged, aggressive dieting can lead to Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport (RED-S), harming health and performance. If you experience chronic fatigue, low libido, or frequent illness, you may need to increase calories even if weight-stable.
