Visualizing the Growth Cycle

See the step-by-step biological process that turns effort in the gym into real muscle. This is what happens inside your body after a challenging workout.

1. The Stimulus

Intense resistance training creates mechanical tension, causing microscopic tears (micro-trauma) in your muscle fibers.

2. The Signal

The damage triggers an inflammatory response. Specialized muscle stem cells (satellite cells) are activated and move toward the damaged sites.

3. The Repair

Fueled by protein, satellite cells fuse to the muscle fibers, donating new nuclei and material to repair and thicken the fiber.

4. The Growth

The fiber is now thicker and stronger than before. This "supercompensation" makes you more resilient to the next training stimulus.
