The Quick Start Guide: Build Your Program

Synthesize all the principles into an actionable plan by following these steps in order. This transforms the theory into your personalized program.

  1. 1

    Set Adherence (Schedule)

    Choose a realistic number of training days (3-5). Select a split that ensures each muscle group is trained at least twice per week (e.g., Upper/Lower, Full Body, Push/Pull/Legs).

  2. 2

    Set VIF (Volume, Intensity, Frequency)

    Based on your training age, assign a weekly set volume (e.g., 10-15 sets per muscle). Distribute these sets across your chosen split. Assign appropriate rep ranges and RPE targets for your goal.

  3. 3

    Plan Progression

    Choose your model. Novice: linear load increases. Intermediate: wave loading for compounds, double progression for isolation. Plan for reactive deloads.

  4. 4

    Select Exercises

    Fill your template with specific exercises. Prioritize compound lifts (Squat, Bench, Deadlift, Press). Add assistance work (Chins, Dips, RDLs) to address weak points. Ensure you can perform them with good, safe form.

  5. 5

    Finalize Rest & Tempo

    Plan for 2.5+ minutes of rest for compounds and 1.5+ for isolation. For tempo, focus on a controlled eccentric and a forceful concentric.
