Cardio Integration: The Strategic Tool

Cardio is not the enemy of muscle, but it must be programmed intelligently. Its primary roles are to improve cardiovascular health and create a caloric deficit for fat loss without excessively impacting recovery from lifting.

HIIT vs. LISS: Choosing Your Weapon

LISS (Low-Intensity Steady-State)

Sustained, low-effort cardio (e.g., incline walking, cycling). Heart Rate: Zone 2 (~60-70% of max).

  • Pros: Low impact on recovery, can be done frequently, aids in recovery by increasing blood flow.
  • Cons: Time-consuming to burn significant calories.

HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training)

Short bursts of all-out effort followed by recovery periods (e.g., sprints, battle ropes). Heart Rate: Zones 4-5 (~80-95%+ of max).

  • Pros: Extremely time-efficient for calorie expenditure.
  • Cons: Highly fatiguing, can interfere with leg day recovery, should be used sparingly (1-2x/week max).

Structured Cardio Protocols

Use these guidelines to program your cardio. Remember, total weekly cardio time should generally not exceed 50% of your total weekly lifting time to minimize interference.

Phase Recommended Type Frequency & Duration
Fat Loss (Cut) Primarily LISS, with optional HIIT 2-4 sessions/week. Start with 20-30 min LISS and add time or one 10-15 min HIIT session as needed.
Muscle Gain (Bulk) LISS 1-2 sessions/week. 20-30 min LISS for cardiovascular health, not for calorie burning.
Maintenance Mix of LISS and HIIT 2-3 sessions/week. E.g., two 30-min LISS sessions and one 15-min HIIT session for overall health and conditioning.
