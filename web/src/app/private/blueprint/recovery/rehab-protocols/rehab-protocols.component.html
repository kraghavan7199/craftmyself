Rehab Protocols for Common Ailments

Crucial Disclaimer

The following are not medical advice or prescriptive rehabilitation plans. They are educational examples of common strategies used to address the muscular imbalances associated with these issues. Always consult a qualified physical therapist or doctor for a proper diagnosis and personalized treatment plan.

Issue: General Lower Back Pain

Often stems from a weak core and glutes, not necessarily a "tight" back. The goal is to build stability around the spine.

  • Focus: Core and glute activation.
  • Example Movements: Bird-Dog (resists rotation), Dead Bug (resists extension), Planks, and Glute Bridges.

Issue: Shoulder Impingement ("Pinched" Feeling)

Typically involves poor scapular control and an imbalance between strong internal rotators (pecs, lats) and weak external rotators.

  • Focus: Strengthening the upper back and rotator cuff.
  • Example Movements: Face Pulls, Band Pull-Aparts, Cable External Rotations, and Wall Slides.

Issue: General Knee Pain (Patellofemoral Pain)

Often related to poor hip stability and weak glute medius, causing the knee to track improperly.

  • Focus: Strengthening the entire hip complex and VMO.
  • Example Movements: Banded Lateral Walks, Clamshells, Step-Downs, and Terminal Knee Extensions (TKEs).
