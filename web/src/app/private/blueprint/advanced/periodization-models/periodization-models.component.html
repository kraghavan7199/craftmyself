Advanced Periodization: Charting the Long Game

Periodization is the logical, long-term organization of training variables to manage fatigue, optimize adaptation, and peak for a specific goal. Here are the most relevant models for the advanced athlete.

Periodization Models Explained

Block Periodization

Training is structured into distinct, sequential blocks, each with a specific focus. This is ideal for peaking for a single event like a competition.

  • Accumulation Block (4-6 wks): High volume, lower intensity. Goal: Build work capacity and muscle mass.
  • Transmutation Block (2-4 wks): Moderate volume, high intensity. Goal: Convert new mass into strength.
  • Realization/Taper (1-2 wks): Low volume, very high intensity. Goal: Shed fatigue and peak for performance.

Undulating Periodization

Training variables (volume, intensity) are varied more frequently, either weekly (WUP) or daily (DUP). Excellent for offseason muscle and strength gain.

  • Example (DUP):
    • Monday: Heavy Squats (Strength, 3x5)
    • Wednesday: Squats (Hypertrophy, 4x10)
    • Friday: Squats (Power, 6x3 w/ explosive speed)
