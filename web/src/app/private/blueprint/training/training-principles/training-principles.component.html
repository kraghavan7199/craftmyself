Training Principles: The Engine of Your Program

Level 2: Volume, Intensity, & Frequency (VIF)

These are the interdependent variables that determine your training stimulus. Finding the right balance is the core of program design.

Volume (How Much)

Tracked as the number of hard sets per muscle group per week. More volume generally equals more growth, but only up to a point.

  • Guideline: 10-20 sets per muscle group/week. Start lower (~10-12) and progress.
  • Be mindful of overlapping volume from compound lifts (e.g., bench press trains chest, shoulders, triceps).

Intensity (How Hard)

Refers to both load (%1RM) and effort (RPE/RIR), or how close you are to failure.

  • For Hypertrophy: Use a wide range, but the bulk of volume (~2/3) is best in the 6-12 rep range.
  • Training to Failure: Use sparingly. It generates high fatigue and is risky on heavy compounds. Most sets should be 1-3 reps shy of failure.

Frequency (How Often)

Frequency is how you organize your weekly volume. Spreading volume out allows for higher quality work.

  • Guideline: Train each muscle group/movement pattern at least twice per week. This is superior to "bro splits".

Level 3: Progression by Training Age

Progressive overload must be applied differently as you become more advanced. Your training age is defined by how long it takes you to make measurable progress.

Novice

Can progress workout-to-workout.

Strategy: Use Linear Progression. This is the simplest and most effective method for beginners. Add a small, consistent amount of weight to your main compound lifts each session (e.g., +5 lbs to the squat, +2.5 lbs to the press). Keep sets/reps the same. Ride this wave of progress for as long as possible.

Intermediate

Can progress week-to-week.

Strategy: Use Linear Periodization (Wave Loading). Cycle reps/load weekly (e.g., Week 1: 3x8, Week 2: 3x7, Week 3: 3x6). Use RPE to guide load.

Advanced

Progresses month-to-month or longer.

Strategy: Use Block Periodization. Structure training into distinct blocks (e.g., Accumulation, Intensification, Taper) to manage high fatigue and peak for performance.

Week 1 3 sets of 8 reps {{'@' }} RPE 8 Establish a baseline load for the wave. RPE 8 means you have ~2 reps in reserve.
Week 2 3 sets of 7 reps {{'@' }} RPE 8 Decrease reps by 1, which should allow for a small load increase (~2.5-5%) while keeping effort the same.
